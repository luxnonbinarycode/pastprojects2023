Our History

Established in 1921, the Rylander Theatre has been entertaining the amazing citizens of Americus for years. The designer of this historic treasure comes from the amazing minds of C.K. Howell of New York and William Saling, also of New York. Before its 40-year closure, the theatre had breathtaking visuals consisting of ornate plaster work, beautiful stencil patterns, and painted murals. After its 40-year closure, in 1999, it was redesigned for $4.8 million dollars, giving the theatre today an aesthetic of 1920's glamour and a modern taste. Also on October 1, 1999, the theatre included the Jimmy Carter Auditorium, named after president Jimmy Carter. The theatre reopened on October 1, 1999 in celebration of Carter's birthday. This magnificent theatre was once known as "The Finest Playhouse South of Atlanta", and as you will see when you come to enjoy our shows, we still hold that title. The auditorium has a total of 600 seats on three levels. The three levels consist of the Orchestra, Balcony, and Gallery. Enjoy your show in a land of the past due to the theatre's impressive vintage Art Deco aesthetic.
